Chapter 5 Pan-Sapiens Anthropocentrism (Part 2)

Luo Binhan was a virgin.

His father, who owned a vast fortune, had married twice and had at least ten "non-legal" partners. These were the ones that had been stable relationships; the ones he'd just slept with, Luo Binhan couldn't even count.

His mother's side was much better. She had only been married three times so far, never had a "non-legal partner" within marriage, and had probably dated five or six boyfriends—the extent of which Luo Binhan absolutely didn't want to ask about.

His parents' behavior had deeply influenced him. He was decent-looking, well-off, but had no girlfriend, no first kiss, and of course no life harmony.

His mother once thought he had a crush on Zhou Yu. Because Zhou Yu came from a clean family and was upright, she was happy about it, fully supportive, and willing to provide legal assistance—until she later discovered that when they shared a blanket, it really was just pure chat. She began to suspect her son had a functional defect.

Luo Binhan had no defect. He reacted very normally to being forcibly kissed by a stunning beauty.

"You calling your mom?" said Jing Huang, disturbed by his screams.

Jing Huang looked grim, his eyelids puffy, hair disheveled. He seemed very tired, as if just woken from deep sleep. Luo Binhan grabbed a tuft of his hair and started shaking him frantically: "Your ship officer molested me!"

"Did she sleep with you?"

"Why are you so dirty-minded?"

Jing Huang pushed his hand away: "She is a Gospel; sleeping with people is basic for her."

"What about kissing?"

"Do you know how many slices of bread you've eaten?"

"Forty-two slices this year," said Luo Binhan. "I've been waiting for someone to ask me that. Also, I've eaten six baguettes."

Jing Huang shoved him back into the chair: "Don't disturb my sleep."

The soft chair floated up again, returning to Aralega, who was leisurely holding a flower and dipping it in a drink.

"You want some candy to calm down?" asked Aralega.

Luo Binhan nodded nervously.

Aralega pulled a small white flower from the vase holding flowers and handed it to Luo Binhan. Seeing this, Luo Binhan politely declined: "Forget it, I don't deserve to live on the same planet as Brother Ma..."

"This is a milk-flavored candy made by the Durand people," said Aralega.

Luo Binhan took the flower skeptically and put it in his mouth. The petals slowly expanded into a gummy soft ball, then melted into sweet milk-flavored juice.

He felt his stomach churning; he hadn't had any food or water for hours since the two fried eggs that morning. This flower candy made him even hungrier.

"You can dip it in some condiment," Aralega suggested.

Luo Binhan pulled out a pale pink flower from the vase himself, dipped it into a dark green drink for a few seconds, leaving emerald droplets on the petals.

This flower tasted like sweet peach crisps coated with cucumber juice.

Luo Binhan suddenly understood. He finally realized that the sour berry chocolate taste lingering in his mouth had nothing to do with Aralega's saliva.

"The captain has many enemies," said Aralega. "One of them was hiding on your planet earlier. The captain went to kill him, but the captain himself is being hunted by other enemies. You took him in, and I am grateful."

She nodded solemnly, the thin metal chains hanging from her horns jingling. Luo Binhan squirmed uncomfortably.

"The one he wanted to kill is dead. Now we plan to leave here, passing through the City of Gates to go beyond the Confederation," said Aralega. "The captain thinks this will prevent his enemies from finding him."

Luo Binhan chewed the petals in contemplation for a few seconds, then asked: "What is the Confederation? Your government?"

"More like your international community, but much looser than that."

"Meaning they can't do anything?"

Aralega smiled cheerfully: "Here in the Farless domain, the Confederation really can't do anything."

"Is there no law in this universe?"

"Better than nothing."

"...Is anyone responsible for enforcing it?"

"It's a dead letter."

"Oh... then how do the masses guarantee daily production and life?"

"Leave it to fate."

Luo Binhan swallowed the candy juice and said: "So how do you make a living?"

Aralega smiled without speaking. So Luo Binhan asked again: "How do you usually split accounts with the authorities?"

"We don't split accounts," said Aralega. "We don't communicate with the authorities."

Luo Binhan pulled the remaining stem from his mouth, cupped his hands to Aralega, and said: "I'm impressed, impressed. So you are the interstellar Mafia? Am I joining as a grunt?"

"No, you don't need to do anything," Aralega replied. "The Silent will protect your safety during this time."

Luo Binhan understood. He was nothing more than an interstellar tourist souvenir plant that Jing Huang had casually grabbed from Blue Star.

"How long until I can go back?"

"That depends on the situation."

"Will my family and friends be okay?"

"The captain thinks now that you've left, they will be."

That was roughly everything Luo Binhan wanted to know about his current situation. He stopped asking questions, leaned back into the soft chair, and for a moment didn't want to care about anything except eating flower candies.

But Aralega had other ideas: "There are three issues we need to resolve. The first is language."

"But you speak Chinese very well," said Luo Binhan. "Does the whole universe speak Chinese?"

"I learned your language from the captain. It belongs to the same language family as the captain's native tongue, so deciphering it was easy. But you still need to learn one or two common languages."

Luo Binhan started to feel uneasy. He often traveled abroad, but his foreign language scores had never been good. "When do we start?"

Aralega gently touched her lips: "You've already learned."

Luo Binhan opened his mouth in bewilderment. He saw Aralega utter a series of syllables from her lips, a language he had definitely never heard before, yet his brain naturally understood.

"Exchanging saliva with me allows partial knowledge sharing," she said. "This is how we Gospel learn."

"...Does it have to be saliva?"

Aralega smiled ambiguously. She licked her lips with her bright red tongue and said: "That's just the basic form, and it has obvious flaws... You can use the language I taught you, but only words for concepts you understand. If something doesn't exist in your mother tongue, it will be replaced with the closest concept."

Luo Binhan didn't understand what she meant, but he wasn't very concerned. All his attention was on a more academic question: "May I ask what the advanced form of your clan's learning is?"

"Reproductive information exchange. For you, sex is enough," Aralega answered without evasion. "That way, the information we have can be fully shared. Want to give it a try?"

"Oh. No thank you," said Luo Binhan. "Then why aren't you called the Welfare clan?" (In chinese it's more like fan service, the two words are similar in Chinese)

"'Gospel' is the matching word you chose yourself."

Luo Binhan firmly believed he had no idea what she was talking about.

"The second issue to resolve," Aralega continued, "you were born in the Trap Zone. That should naturally make you adapted to Rational civilization lifestyles, but in the upcoming journey, we will encounter Testamental civilizations. You might encounter situations you cannot understand. I hope you won't panic."

"Because nothing will really happen?"

"Because you are powerless against it," Aralega said tenderly.

Luo Binhan bit into another flower: "Alright, then I'll just die. Anything else?"

Aralega said: "We will arrange for someone to accompany you until you adapt to life on the ship."

Luo Binhan thought for a moment, then asked solemnly: "Will that person sleep with me?"

Aralega seemed to pause for a few seconds. She wasn't quite sure.

"He is not Gospel clan," she said. "Hmm, but if you both want, I don't think anyone would object..."

She didn't use Chinese, so Luo Binhan could tell the "he" she is refering to was male. Although learning foreign languages had been painful for him since middle school, after weighing it, he felt this wasn't a viable reason to sacrifice his body.

"I won't sleep with him, he won't sleep with me," he said righteously.

Aralega soon brought over the companion who would not sleep with him and he would not sleep with.
