Chapter 4: Pan-Sapiens Anthropocentrism (Part 1)

Luo Binhan was a man with considerable experience traveling abroad.

In his suitcase prepared for his planned trip abroad, he had packed three sets of a change of clothes, two pairs of socks, a toiletry kit, several boxes of specialty snacks from Pear Sea City, a phone charger and plug adapter, exchanged cash, a passport, a water bottle, and a pack of napkins. Additionally, in his pockets were his phone, wallet, and a lighter. The wallet contained credit cards, change, a keychain, ID documents, and a few one-inch photos.

About the lighter: Luo Binhan sometimes—such as when he learned that his unlucky little sister had caused a string of troubles—had a habit of smoking, but not a strong addiction. Carrying a lighter was more of a hobby of his. It was a rather extravagant silver lighter, finely engraved with arabesque patterns. On the day Luo Binhan came of age, Zhou Yu had given it to him as a coming-of-age gift, back when Zhou Yu's childhood sweetheart still hadn't disappeared.

Thinking about this, Luo Binhan felt a bit baffled. He didn't intend to place blame on Zhou Yu, but he nonetheless felt that his friend's luck was rather poor. Zhou Yu had lost his childhood sweetheart—who could well be called his child bride—a year ago, and now his best friend was about to be taken away by a UFO descending from the sky. What an unfortunate thing that would be for that reclusive medical student. Was this the end? Would Zhou Yu have to spend his life accompanied by a parrot?

But now was not the time to consider Zhou Yu and the parrot.

Luo Binhan and all his personal belongings were brought to a circular hall. It looked like a fairly normal rest area, with soft chairs, bookshelves, and a television (at least it looked like one), and there were even several glowing potted plants on the table. The entire circular hall was brightly lit, but the light source was untraceable. Embedded in the very center of the hall's floor was a large sheet of transparent glass, beneath which stretched the deep, boundless void of space. It seemed to be a cosmic version of a glass skybridge.

Luo Binhan cautiously skirted around that area with a touch of reverence. From the stars streaking backward, he judged that the spacecraft was moving at high speed. Where was the sun? He had no memory of when the ship had passed that massive fireball.

Just then, a green-haired person drifted over. He said to Luo Binhan: "You know the floor is a simulated image, right?"

"Huh?"

"A simulated image." The green-haired person emphasized. "You know what? The Silent is a marvelously designed ship. It definitely wouldn't put the bridge directly against the hull. Actually, there's power equipment beneath us... By the way, what do you think of the flower arrangement on the table? Do you think it's better to have all the calyxes tilted toward one direction as they are now, or to arrange them symmetrically based on the parity of their petals?"

"What?"

"Arrangement, arrangement. Some statistics claim that the placement of living matter contains intrinsic order, and it often reveals certain unconscious traits of the population living here. Ah, that's not important." The green-haired person said cheerfully. "Color, I think color is more important. Which shade of green do you prefer? #006030 or #009100? By the way, how can the coding system you use to distinguish colors be so simplistic? Is it really sufficient? There must be plenty of things that are insufficient in your world, aren't there? Can you list the top ten most insufficient things on your planet? How do you view the lack of material existence on the surface of rocky planets?"

Luo Binhan said politely: "Hello and goodbye."

The green-haired person was about to speak again when Jing Huang walked straight over and kicked him. The kick didn't land solidly but passed straight through his body.

"Hey, helm, go steer the ship."

The green-haired person floated up under Luo Binhan's astonished gaze. With a wave of his hand, he changed his hair to purple: "Captain, I want to express my dissatisfaction. My name is not helm. My name is '∈'—ah, in your culture, this mathematical symbol is quite common, you know? Do you know that there are linguistic divisions on your planet? When you see it, does it evoke any imagination about foreign languages?"

Mid-sentence, '∈' suddenly turned back to Luo Binhan, extended a finger in front of his eyes, and wrote a glowing symbol in the air: ∈.

Jing Huang glanced at the symbol and then said with certainty: "You steer the ship, so you're the helm. So less nonsense, go steer the ship."

The purple-haired '∈' vanished on the spot with a plaintive look. Seeing this, Luo Binhan directly grabbed Jing Huang by the collar: "...Is he a person or a ghost?"

Jing Huang pried his hand off: "Information aggregate mind branch-stream."

"Speak human language!"

"What you call artificial intelligence." Jing Huang said impatiently. "Don't mind him. Branch streams almost never have fixed personality data. He babbles on all day long, annoyingly loud."

Luo Binhan thought of his parrot again. That parrot used to be quite close to Jing Huang too; it sat in its cage every day shouting 'Captain, captain' and vigorously shaking its bird swing.

"Can I go back now?" he asked Jing Huang with a grave expression.

"Go back my ass," Jing Huang said. "If you go back, you'll die."

Luo Binhan was in despair, profoundly feeling that his life was a failure, that these aliens were a failure, and that the entire universe was a failure.

Jing Huang pressed him into an egg-shaped soft chair and then said: "Aralega, handle this. I'm going to sleep."

"Sleep my ass!" Luo Binhan reached out to grab his hair. "Spill everything!"

Luo Binhan fully expected to catch hold of him easily like before, but this time Jing Huang slipped downward. Luo Binhan clicked his tongue watching this, and only a few seconds later realized it was his own seat that was rising.

The egg-shaped soft chair rose to the ceiling. Several small trays emerged from the seamless walls, holding cups of drinks and delicate flower arrangements. The flowers resembled oxalis a bit, but were more colorful and lovely. Luo Binhan carefully picked up the drink that looked most like plain water. Just then, another soft chair floated up from below.

In the chair sat a woman. At the sight of her, Luo Binhan immediately became spellbound, nearly dropping his water glass.

She had golden-brown hair, twisted into an elaborate bun at the back of her head, adorned with tiny flowers. Her bronze skin was tender and glowing, as if coated with a layer of honey. She wore a tight top that was barely more than a bra, with glowing tattoo patterns on her exposed collarbones and abdomen.

This woman could rank among the top three beauties Luo Binhan had ever seen. Her dark complexion and hot figure had a unique wildness. But that wasn't her most special feature.

Two large, curly, pitch-black goat-like horns protruded from her thick hair. Below her leather pants, solid and smooth thighs were exposed, and from the knees down, her legs were covered with brown fur, extending to her feet—which were not dainty jade feet, but thick, dark black hooves.

The horned and hoofed woman gracefully bent down and picked up a small purple flower from the table, biting into it. As she ate the flower, she said: "Hello, I'm Aralega, first mate of The Silent."

Her voice was low, husky, and magnetic. Luo Binhan felt an electric shiver run from the soles of his feet to the tips of his hair. This woman's voice was like a feather, tickling his insides and making them itch. Luo Binhan clearly recognized that feeling as a flutter of the heart; the last time he had felt such a stir was in junior high school.

So he said with deep emotion: "Can you stay away from me?"

The goat-horned woman Aralega tilted her head, gazing at him with seductive charm. A stray lock of hair curled like a flower vine, gently swaying by her ear. Every little move she made was so graceful and enticing that Luo Binhan couldn't help adding: "With horns so heavy on your head, doesn't your neck get tired?"

"My skull is one millimeter thicker than yours, my cervical vertebrae are two millimeters wider in diameter, I have six more vertebrae, and my muscle distribution is different," Aralega said. "I am physiologically built to bear more weight."

"Are you a half-beast?"

Aralega swallowed the flower stem. "No? First, you'd have to define what half-beast is."

Luo Binhan gave up thinking. He asked: "What exactly do you people want?"

"To protect you," Aralega picked another flower. "At least, that's what the captain says."

"Why do I need protection?"

Aralega began dipping the flowers into the drinks. "Because you've gotten involved with the captain. The captain has a bunch of enemies who are skilled at assassination. They kill anyone who gets involved with him."

Luo Binhan nodded calmly, leaned over to the side of the chair, and shouted down with a middle finger: "Jing Huang, I curse on your ancestors!"

Then he turned to Aralega and said: "Go on."

Aralega gently bit a flower petal with her white teeth, her lips blooming into a seductive smile. She said: "I'll explain in the simplest way possible."

Luo Binhan was about to speak again when Aralega stood up from her chair. She wiped her lips with her crystal-studded nails, then bent down, wrapped her arms around Luo Binhan's neck, and gave him a long, sweet kiss.
