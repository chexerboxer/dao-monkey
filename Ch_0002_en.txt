Luo Binhan was completely stunned. He had no idea what she was talking about. But at least he recognized this thing—he had bought a set for his sister before, and knew it could really electrocute someone until even their mother wouldn't recognize them. So he immediately stopped screaming and clamped his mouth shut.

The beautiful nun pressed the tip of the stun gun harder against his throat.

"Where is the Degradation Hunter!?"

Luo Binhan felt the cold pressure of metal against his throat, making it hard to breathe. He began to miss the dog he used to have, but that dog was long gone, and where it was now he couldn't say.

He felt despair. Just then, the door to the side hall opened. Luo Binhan twisted his head desperately to look and saw another four or five nuns enter. They were all so beautiful that it made him even more despairing, and without a word, they began to undress.

Six beautiful nuns in skimpy light armor surrounded him.

"Is this him?" asked a nun with a prominent chest.

"He's in the 0206 records," replied another whose waist was particularly sparing of fabric.

Although he didn't know what they were talking about, Luo Binhan resolutely shouted, "Mistake! All a mistake! You've got the wrong person!"

The nuns were not moved by his soulful performance. The palest-skinned nun put her hand on her hip and sauntered gracefully around the room before making a decision: "Take him away. Interrogate him slowly."

"Wait, please wait," Luo Binhan said calmly. "I have one last thing to say."

The nuns all fixed their star-like beautiful eyes on him. Luo Binhan took a deep breath and said to the nun with the most prominent chest, "Once there was a sincere love placed before me, but I did not treasure it. The most painful thing in the human world is nothing more than this. You resemble that woman in some ways, making me fall in love at first sight. If possible, I wish to elope with you. If I must set a time limit on this execution, I hope it is now, immediately, right away."

"I don't love you," the nun replied without batting an eye.

Luo Binhan immediately roared with all his might.

"Pervert lingerie nuns are abducting a good young man! Someone help me! Ahhhhhhh!!!"

Just then, the door to the side hall opened again. Luo Binhan looked over hopefully, only to find another beautiful nun. Possibly disturbed by Luo Binhan's screams, her expression wasn't very pleasant.

The other nuns looked at her strangely.

"Miya, you should be watching outside," said the palest-skinned nun.

The nun called Miya didn't take off her clothes. She collapsed without a word, revealing a young man in a red robe behind her.

"You're too noisy," the young man said contemptuously to Luo Binhan.

That voice ignited Luo Binhan's hope. He recognized the owner of the voice—his name was Jing Huang, a strange young man he had met by chance a few months ago. The young man had many unsolved mysteries, but none of that mattered now.

Jing Huang kicked the fallen nun and walked slowly into the hall. With more mechanical clicks, the nuns turned in unison to face him. Luo Binhan was tied up tightly and couldn't see what they were pulling out, but frankly, he didn't really want to know.

"Shoot," the young man said expressionlessly.

"Aim carefully," added Luo Binhan, who could only sit straight in front of the nuns.

The nuns opened fire. Luo Binhan thought that hearing explosions so close might deafen him, but the sound from behind him wasn't a loud boom; it was a very short chirping sound. He almost thought it was birdsong.

Luo Binhan saw countless thin red rays shoot from behind him. They flew past his scalp toward the side hall door, weaving into a bright red grid that sizzled on the floor. Scared, he instinctively closed his eyes.

So he couldn't say much about what happened next. He felt a scorching wind blow from behind him. The gale raged through the entire hall, sending the furnishings flying everywhere. Luo Binhan sensed something solid moving around him—calling it moving because it was very agile, stopping and flying around him like a fish swimming in the air.

He dared to open his eyes. A series of black shadows shot past his face.

The side hall was unrecognizable, on fire everywhere. The nuns lay on the floor, hands and feet covered in blood. Luo Binhan almost thought they were dead, but when a flame licked one of their fingers, she convulsed in pain.

The red-robed young man stood in the center of the hall, stepping on the palest-skinned nun. He kicked her, forcing her to look at him.

"...Xuanhong's Jade," the nun said.

The young man kicked her again: "Who told you to speak? What do you want with 0312?"

His kick wasn't heavy, but the nun writhed in pain. Jing Huang coldly grabbed her hair and pressed his right index finger to her forehead.

The nun began to scream as if electrocuted. Her eyes rolled back, and her creamy skin emerged with patches of burnt black spots.

Jing Huang clicked his tongue, withdrew his hand, and threw her back to the ground. Then he walked toward Luo Binhan. Only then did Luo Binhan realize that the shackles binding him had been cut into several pieces. He quickly jumped up from the chair.

"Hey, Jing Huang, these women—"

"Not dead, just broken limbs. They'll be fine after lying down for a while."

The young man pushed him expressionlessly out of the hall. Although Luo Binhan was eager to leave, he couldn't help looking back.

"Where have you been for the past three months..."

"City of Gates."

"Huh?"

"You wouldn't know even if I told you. I was planning to go farther, but halfway I heard those people were coming. The arrays you set up at home couldn't stop them, so I decided to take you away first."

Luo Binhan was a bit dazed, so he changed the subject: "Were those women looking for you?"

"No, the ones looking for me haven't arrived yet. They came to kill 0312."

The 0312 Jing Huang mentioned was another strange man Luo Binhan had met a few months ago. The man claimed his surname was Fake and his given name Ke, working as a programmer in a tech company in the neighboring city. His most striking feature was a bald head as glaring as a monk's.

Like Jing Huang, Fake had disappeared three months ago, right around the time Luo Binhan's friend Zhou Yu was discharged from the hospital. But at the time, Luo Binhan wasn't surprised; he knew both Jing Huang and Fake had secrets different from ordinary people.

That was—

"Get in the car," Jing Huang said.

Luo Binhan stared at the flashy red sports car in front of him. The joy of reunion vanished instantly.

"Jing Huang!" he roared. "You took my fucking car again!"

"This is my car."

"Bullshit! You don't even have a driver's license!"

The young man frowned impatiently: "What are you shouting about? I said it's mine, so it's mine. No time to explain. Get in the car!"

He kicked Luo Binhan and his suitcase into the back seat, then moved to the driver's side and started the engine. Luo Binhan gasped as the suitcase hit him, barely managing to sit up. He was about to argue when he saw the scenery outside the car and forgot everything in fear.

The sports car was speeding down the city streets at an estimated speed of over eighty kilometers per hour. But strangely, the engine sounded silent.

Eighty kilometers wasn't an exact estimate, and the dashboard seemed broken, motionless. But based on his experience driving on highways, Luo Binhan knew the actual speed was only higher. He almost screamed again—compared to the laser guns of those pervert lingerie nuns, hitting anything at this speed in the city would be a more miserable death. And perhaps dying instantly would be better than surviving the crash.

"Slow the fuck dow—huh?"

The car jolted slightly, the front wheels tilting upward noticeably. Was there a ramp on this city road? Before Luo Binhan could figure it out, the familiar feeling of weightlessness enveloped him. Right before his eyes, the car's rear wheels left the ground, and the whole car flew into the air like a glider.

To be honest, this wasn't Luo Binhan's first time experiencing something like this. Silently, he stared up at the blue sky for a moment, then pulled out his phone and called Zhou Yu.

"I'm ascending to heaven," he said calmly. "Remember to feed my parrot."

On the other end of the line, Zhou Yu said, "Huh?"
